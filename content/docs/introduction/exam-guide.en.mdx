## Purpose of the Exam

The **SAA-C03** exam validates your ability to:

- **Design** new solutions and architectures on AWS
- **Review** existing architectures and determine if they need improvements
- Optimize across the **four pillars of the Well-Architected Framework**: security, resiliency, performance, and cost

## Target Candidate

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| Ideal experience | **At least 1 year** of hands-on experience with AWS |
| Alternative | A **project portfolio** can substitute for formal work experience |
| Technical requirements | No coding or operational experience required |
| Prior knowledge | **Cloud Practitioner** level (foundational) |

<Callout type="idea" title="Instructor's advice">
After completing the course, build a portfolio. Practical projects show employers you can apply what you've learned — don't underestimate their value.
</Callout>

## Exam Format

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Exam code** | SAA-C03 |
| **Total questions** | 65 (50 scored + 15 unscored pilot questions) |
| **Time limit** | 130 minutes |
| **Multiple choice** | Single correct answer (not explicitly labeled) |
| **Multiple response** | Two or more correct answers (will say "select two" or more) |
| **Passing score** | **720 / 1000** |

<Callout type="warn" title="Practice target: aim for 90%">
The actual passing threshold varies slightly based on exam difficulty. Aiming for **90% on mock exams** gives you a comfortable safety margin. If you're scoring in the 80s in practice, you'll likely pass — but don't get overconfident.
</Callout>

## Exam Domains

These are the **four areas** that make up 100% of the exam. Notice how **security carries the most weight**:

| Domain | Content | Weight | Focus Areas |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Secure Architectures** | **30%** | IAM, encryption, access control, firewalls, network security |
| 2 | **Resilient Architectures** | **26%** | High availability, fault tolerance, disaster recovery |
| 3 | **High-Performing Architectures** | **24%** | Scalability, caching, compute and storage optimization |
| 4 | **Cost-Optimized Architectures** | **20%** | Right-sizing, Reserved/Spot instances, budgets, auto scaling |

<Callout type="info" title="Key change in SAA-C03 vs previous versions">
Domain 4 (cost) decreased in weight compared to earlier versions. In contrast, **Domain 3 now includes a new task statement**: designing **data ingestion and transformation solutions** (data pipelines).
</Callout>

## Data Pipelines and ML Services

Although this is an architecture exam, SAA-C03 **includes questions about data pipelines**. This means services like the following are in scope:

| Service | Role in the Pipeline |
|---|---|
| **Kinesis** | Real-time streaming data ingestion |
| **Glue** | Serverless ETL — extract, transform, and load |
| **Athena** | Serverless SQL queries on data in S3 |
| **Redshift** | Data warehouse for large-scale analytics |
| **SageMaker** | ML model training and deployment |

## Question Style

The exam is **scenario-based**, not memorization. You won't be asked "What is a VPC?" but rather:

> *"A company has EC2 instances in private subnets that need to download patches from the internet without being directly accessible from it. Which solutions would you choose?"*

You'll need to connect multiple services and apply design criteria (security, resiliency, cost, performance) to choose the correct answer.

<Callout type="success" title="Study summary">
- **65 questions**, 130 minutes, minimum **720/1000** to pass
- **Security = 30%** of the exam — the heaviest domain
- Questions are **scenario-based**, not recall
- Includes **data pipelines** (Kinesis, Glue, Athena) — don't skip them
- Aim for **90%+ on mock exams** for a safety margin
</Callout>
