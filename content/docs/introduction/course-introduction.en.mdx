## Course Objectives

The primary goal of this course is to teach you to **design architectures on AWS** applying the four pillars of the Well-Architected Framework. By the end, you should be able to answer questions like:

| Pillar | Typical Exam Question |
|---|---|
| **Security** | How do I improve security for EC2 instances in a public VPC? |
| **Reliability** | How do I improve resilience for a Direct Connect connection? |
| **Performance** | How do I optimize read performance for DynamoDB? |
| **Cost Optimization** | How do I reduce costs without losing functionality? |

## Prerequisites

<Callout type="warn" title="Requirement: Cloud Practitioner level">
This course assumes you have **Cloud Practitioner (CCP) level** knowledge. This can come from:
- A **passed CCP certification**, or
- **Relevant experience** working with AWS in production

If you can't pass the Cloud Practitioner assessment found in this course, **go back and complete the CCP course first**.
</Callout>

| Do you have this? | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Passed CCP certification | Continue directly with SAA |
| Hands-on AWS production experience | Try the CCP mock exam; if you pass (≥70%), continue |
| Neither of the above | Take the Cloud Practitioner course first |

<Callout type="info" title="Same instructors">
**Sanjeev Thiyagarajan** and **Michael Forrester** also teach the Cloud Practitioner course at KodeKloud. If you need to take it first, the experience will be consistent.
</Callout>

## Course Structure

The course is divided into **2 mega-sections**, plus assessments at the beginning and end:

### Mega-Section 1: Meet Your Services

Instructor: **Sanjeev Thiyagarajan**

Covers approximately **100-150 AWS services** organized into 9 categories. Each category includes theoretical lectures, hands-on demos, and a **review quiz** at the end.

| # | Category | Example Services |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Networking | VPC, Subnets, Route Tables, NAT Gateway |
| 2 | Storage | S3, EBS, EFS, FSx |
| 3 | Compute | EC2, Lambda, ECS, EKS |
| 4 | Database | RDS, Aurora, DynamoDB, ElastiCache |
| 5 | Application Integration | SQS, SNS, Step Functions, EventBridge |
| 6 | Data & ML | Kinesis, Glue, Athena, SageMaker |
| 7 | Migration & Transfer | DMS, Snow Family, DataSync |
| 8 | Management & Governance | CloudWatch, CloudTrail, Config |
| 9 | Security | IAM, KMS, WAF, Shield |

### Mega-Section 2: Design for X

Instructor: **Michael Forrester**

**Scenario-based questions** where you apply the four pillars to the services you already know:

| Module | Theme | What You'll Learn |
|---|---|---|
| Design for **Security** | Encryption, access, networking | Protecting data at rest and in transit, IAM policies |
| Design for **Reliability** | HA, DR, fault tolerance | Multi-AZ, Auto Scaling, disaster recovery strategies |
| Design for **Performance** | Scaling, caching, efficiency | CloudFront, ElastiCache, instance right-sizing |
| Design for **Cost** | Reservations, lifecycle, spot | Reserved Instances, S3 lifecycle, Spot Instances |

<Callout type="idea" title="Design Challenge">
After completing the 4 design modules, you'll face the **Ultimate Design Challenge**: an integrative scenario combining all pillars. It's your final test before the mock exam.
</Callout>

## Recommended Study Strategies

| Strategy | Detail | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Take notes per service** | Write each service on its own page as you learn it | Handwriting improves long-term retention |
| **Be consistent: 15 min/day** | Study a little every day instead of sporadic marathons | Spaced learning is more effective than cramming |
| **Practice with labs and playgrounds** | Use the course environments to experiment | Active practice consolidates better than passive theory |
| **Protect your study time** | Block a fixed schedule and eliminate distractions | Deep focus accelerates comprehension |

<Callout type="warn" title="Plan ~8 weeks of study">
With **15 consistent minutes per day**, most students are exam-ready in approximately **8 weeks**. If you study more per day, you can shorten this, but consistency matters more than intensity.
</Callout>
