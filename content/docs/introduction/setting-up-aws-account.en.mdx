# Setting Up Your Own AWS Account

## Why You Need a Personal Account

Playgrounds cover most exam services, but not all. A personal account gives you full access to explore **any service without restrictions**.

## Rule #1 (Critical): Don't Leave Anything Running

> **⚠️ Always stop or delete resources when you finish a study session.**

This includes: NAT Gateways, Elastic IPs, storage volumes, EC2 instances, etc. Forgetting to clean up is the most common cause of unexpected charges.

## Email Tip

If you use Gmail or Yahoo, use the **"+" alias trick**:
- `yourname+aws1@gmail.com` → creates a unique AWS account that still arrives in your main inbox.

## Signup Process

1. Go to AWS and click **"Create a free account"**.
2. Provide an email address, password, and account name.
3. Enter a **valid credit card** (required even on the free tier).
4. Verify your identity by phone.
5. Select the **Free Support Plan (Basic Support)** — no paid support needed as a student.

## After Registration

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Activation | The account may take up to **24 hours** to fully activate globally |
| Avoid Root | Create an **IAM User** with admin rights and use it for all logins |
| AWS Budgets | Set a **$5–$10 budget alert** to be notified before incurring significant charges |
